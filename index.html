How it works

ParaSwap in three steps

Start with the token pair you want, compare the live route, then approve the swap from your own wallet without handing custody to an exchange account.

1

Connect your wallet

Open the app and connect a Web3 wallet. Your keys stay with you, and every approval is signed locally.

2

Choose your swap

Select ETH, USDC or another supported token, then let the app compare available DeFi liquidity paths.

3

Review and sign

Check the quoted output, route, slippage controls, gas estimate and approval request before confirming the transaction from your wallet.

FAQ

ParaSwap questions

01What is ParaSwap?

ParaSwap is a DeFi swap aggregator known for comparing token routes across decentralized liquidity sources. The current product identity redirects through Velora, while the core search intent remains wallet-based swap execution.

02Is ParaSwap non-custodial?

Yes. You connect your wallet, review approvals and sign transactions yourself. The app does not need to take custody of your assets for a swap.

03Which chains and assets are supported?

Common swap routes cover assets such as ETH, stablecoins and many EVM tokens across networks including Polygon, Optimism and Arbitrum. See the full list on the supported chains page.

04How long does a swap take?

Timing depends on the selected chain, wallet confirmation and transaction inclusion. The live status and expected flow are shown in the app before and after signing.

05What does it cost, and how should I check safety?

You pay the token approval cost when needed, network gas and any route-level costs shown before signing. Check the quote, token address, permissions and slippage settings before approving a trade.
